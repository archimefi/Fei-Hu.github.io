### Overview
With application of a discharge current of 65 mA in a commercial primary coin cell (CR2032), a maximum temperature difference of **7.9 °C** was measured between the cell separator and external packaging via upconverting nanoparticle thermometry.

### Key contributions
- Be the first one to apply upconverting nanoparticle thermometry into batteries.
- A huge temperature gradient from cathode, separator to anode was elucidated.
- The introduction of upconverting nanoparticles into electrode/separator has negligible effect on electrochemistry.
- Shows a large temperature difference between the cell separator and external packaging, revealing the importance of focusing internal instead of external temperature of batteries.
